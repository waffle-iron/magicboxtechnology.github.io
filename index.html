General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

The European Commission has published a data protection package to reform, modernise and harmonise European data protection law. The cornerstone of the package is the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) which will replace the 1995 Data Protection Directive and, in the UK, the Data Protection Act 1998.

GDPR has been designed to allow consumers easier access to their own proprietary data and empower them to control access. It will replace the current Data Protection Act (DPA) but will be specific to any company holding European personal data whether based in Europe or the rest of the world.

When is this happening?

The new GDPR legislation will come into force from May 2018.

What is my liability/risk?

€20 million or 4% of global annual turnover for the preceding financial year.

The GDPR will apply in the UK from 25 May 2018. The government has confirmed that the UK’s decision to leave the EU will not affect the commencement of the GDPR.
